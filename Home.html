What is Intersubjectivity?

Intersubjectivity refers to the shared understanding and mutual awareness between individuals. It is the space where personal experiences, meanings, and perspectives intersect, allowing people to connect, communicate, and build relationships. It highlights how meaning is not formed in isolation but through dialogue and interaction.

Philosophers on Intersubjectivity

Confucius

Confucius emphasized harmony in relationships and the importance of empathy and ritual in fostering mutual respect. His thought implies that intersubjectivity arises from shared social practices and moral cultivation.

Martin Buber

Buber distinguished between two modes of relating: the "I-It" (objectifying) and the "I-Thou" (relational). For him, true intersubjectivity occurs in the I-Thou encounter, where individuals meet authentically, acknowledging the other as a whole being.

Karol Wojtyła (Pope John Paul II)

Wojtyła viewed intersubjectivity through the lens of personalism, stressing the dignity of the human person. He argued that authentic human existence is realized in communion with others, not in isolation.
